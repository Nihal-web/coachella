Coachella: 20 Years In The Desert

Streaming free on YouTube 

YouTube Originals

YouTube Originals and Coachella have joined forces to produce a full-length documentary titled "Coachella: Two Decades in the Desert." Stream it exclusively on YouTube today.

This documentary, in the making for two decades, delves into the festival's archives for the first time, unveiling the performances and behind-the-scenes anecdotes that have molded the music extravaganza. It offers a rare glimpse into Coachella's vibrant origins, showcases exclusive, never-before-seen footage and interviews, and highlights pivotal performances by music industry giants such as Billie Eilish, Kanye West, Daft Punk, Travis Scott, BLACKPINK, LCD Soundsystem, Rage Against The Machine, Pixies, Swedish House Mafia, Jane’s Addiction, Björk, The White Stripes, Madonna, Moby, Beck, Radiohead, and many more

1999

Main Stage

 The inaugural Coachella festival took place beneath the October sky, with palm trees adorning the horizon behind the main stage, setting the stage for the festival's inception.

2001

jane's addiction

Following an almost four-year hiatus, the iconic band joined once more to take center stage at Coachella.

Photo by Zach Cordner

2002

Björk

The Icelandic singer and songwriter graced the stage as a headlining act while several months pregnant.

Photo by Brian Lima

2003

The White Stripes

In its initial years, Coachella celebrated rock and indie bands as the ultimate stars. The White Stripes, led by Jack & Meg White, tore up the stage.

Photo by Brian Moghadam


2004 

Campgrounds

Annually, thousands flock to Indio for a weekend of music, retreating to their tents as the night draws to a close, seeking rest beneath the star

2005

M.I.A.

 M.I.A debuted at Coachella in 2005 with the first of several electrifying performances. In 2009, she made a memorable return, inciting mayhem by inviting fans to rush the stage and join her in a wild party.

Photo by Brian Moghadam

2006

Daft Punk

The Coachella performance by the renowned electronic producer duo introduced their iconic pyramid stage for the first time. This groundbreaking show set a fresh benchmark for production technology in the realm of electronic dance music.

Image Credit: Zach Cordner

2007

Amy Winehouse

Despite being listed at the bottom of the billing poster, the late English sensation, Amy Winehouse, drew one of the largest crowds of the weekend at Coachella. This marked a pivotal moment in the festival's history, where attendees were not just there for the major headliners but also to discover and appreciate the lesser-known acts.

Image Credit: Brian Moghadam

2008

Roger Waters

The iconic guitarist from the renowned band Pink Floyd delivered two performances at Coachella. During one of his sets, a colossal inflatable pig, a part of his elaborate production, broke free and drifted away into the Indio night, only to be found later in a nearby resident's yard.

Image Credit: Jeff Kravtiz

2009

The Serpent Mother

The art installations at Coachella have transformed year after year. In 2009, the Flaming Lotus Girls crafted a large scale, kinetic fire serpent mother coiled around her egg.

Photo: Zach Cordner
